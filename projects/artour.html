Project Overview

AR Tour is an augmented reality geolocation application for iOS that allows users to experience stories on-site in a novel way. The project consists of two key components: historical site explorations and a social AR platform called "Reactions" that enhances connection to public spaces.

When I walked past the monumental Dakota building, I didn't even recognize it. From the outside, it might appear to be just another impressive building. Yet behind its walls lie layers of culture, life, and even death - hidden from the human eye.

AR Tour bridges past and present, revealing the hidden layers of history and human experience in the world around us. By placing historical images in their original locations through augmented reality, users can step back in time and see places as they once were. The social "Reactions" feature adds another dimension, allowing people to share emotions and thoughts tied to specific locations.

Featured Experiences

The Dakota Building & John Lennon's Story

The Dakota building experience provides a window into one of New York's most famous landmarks and the tragic events that occurred there. Users can:

  • Learn about the Dakota's history as a haven for creative minds, most notably John Lennon and Yoko Ono
  • Experience a recreation of the tragic night of John Lennon's murder
  • View historical images overlaid on the present-day building
  • Explore Strawberry Fields in Central Park, created as a memorial to Lennon

Reactions: Social AR at Cornell Tech

Inspired by the overwhelming experience of the first day of school, "Reactions" transforms the Cornell Tech campus into an interactive playground where people can share emotions with friends in public spaces.

Technical Implementation

AR Tour leverages cutting-edge technology to deliver compelling location-based experiences:

Google Geospatial
Unity AR
iOS ARKit
ElevenLabs

The core technical stack includes:

  • Unity with AR Foundation: Providing the development environment for creating augmented reality experiences
  • Google AR & VR Geospatial Creator: Enabling precise placement of virtual content in real-world locations
  • ElevenLabs Voice Synthesis: Delivering high-quality narration for historical stories and information
  • Cloud Backend: Storing and serving user-generated reactions and shared content

One of the key technical challenges was ensuring accurate geospatial anchoring of virtual content, particularly in urban environments where GPS signals can be affected by tall buildings. The solution combines Google's Geospatial Creator with additional visual positioning techniques to enhance accuracy.

AR Features & Innovation

Temporal Overlay
Historical images precisely aligned with present-day locations, allowing users to see through time and compare past with present.
Narrative Visualization
Recreations of historical events in their actual locations, bringing stories to life where they actually happened.
Social Persistence
Reactions and comments from other users remain anchored to their locations, creating a persistent social layer on the physical world.
Guided Discovery
Curated paths through cities that connect related stories and experiences, encouraging exploration.

User Experience Design

AR Tour is designed to be intuitive and immersive, even for users who are new to augmented reality. The experience flow follows several key principles:

Location Discovery
Users can find nearby experiences through a map interface or receive notifications when approaching points of interest.
Contextual Activation
AR content activates automatically when users are in the right position, minimizing technical friction.
Multi-Sensory Narration
Audio narration enriches the visual experience, allowing users to maintain visual focus on the AR content.
Intuitive Interaction
Simple gestures allow users to place reactions or interact with historical elements without complex menus.

The design challenges specific to location-based AR included:

  • Creating experiences that work in varying lighting and weather conditions
  • Ensuring content remains accessible even in crowded public spaces
  • Designing interfaces that handle both the AR view and supplementary information
  • Maintaining user awareness of their physical surroundings for safety

Impact & Future Development

AR Tour demonstrates the potential of augmented reality to transform how we experience and interact with physical spaces:

  • Educational Value: Making history more accessible and engaging by placing it in its original context
  • Social Connection: Creating new forms of spatial social media that connect people through shared experiences
  • Cultural Preservation: Documenting and visualizing histories that might otherwise be forgotten
  • Enhancing Public Spaces: Adding new layers of meaning and interaction to everyday environments

Future development plans include:

  • Expanding to additional historically significant locations across New York City and beyond
  • Developing a platform for user-generated content, allowing communities to create their own AR tours
  • Enhancing the social features with more interaction types and persistent virtual modifications
  • Integrating with other platforms and services to create a more comprehensive urban exploration experience

As AR technology continues to advance with devices like Apple Vision Pro and Meta Quest, the potential for more immersive and detailed experiences will only grow, making AR Tour a foundation for future development in location-based augmented reality.
