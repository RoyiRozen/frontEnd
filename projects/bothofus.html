The Both of Us VR Film

2020
VR Creative Video 3D Spatial Design

Project Brief

The Both of Us is a 10-minute science fiction VR drama, my MFA final project, exploring human connection in a technological dystopia. In a world where humanity is addicted to escapist VR glasses controlled by the TCR corporation, two friends, Omar and Mark, remnants of an underground resistance, reunite to eliminate TCR. The film, written and directed as a narrative VR experience, challenges viewers to question their relationship with technology. My role included writing, directing, and leading VR development.

"It's a bit difficult from the shock and the intensity of the creation. I can feel it all over my body—the emotions of the actress, the feelings you wanted to convey. It's amazing to think you've chosen to use technology to criticize the same technology you are using—a choice of a genius."

Cinematic VR & Technology

This narrative VR film blended traditional filmmaking with cutting-edge VR tech. Scenes were captured using photogrammetry (laser scanners, drones, cameras) to create detailed 3D environments. Key technologies included VR development in Unity, 3D modeling, and custom shaders for the dystopian atmosphere. Volumetric capture of actors allowed for multi-angle viewing, enhancing the immersive cinematic storytelling.

Creative Approach & Narrative

The film uses VR to critique the technology it employs, creating a meta-narrative about our relationship with technology. Immersing the audience in a dystopian world dominated by VR invites reflection on technology's seductive power and potential dangers. The narrative structure leverages VR's unique capabilities, allowing viewers to experience the story from multiple perspectives for a personalized experience.

Behind the Scenes & Impact

"The Both of Us" was recognized for its innovative VR storytelling and exploration of human connection. As my MFA project, it represents my research into immersive storytelling. The project has been screened at VR film festivals, contributing to conversations about VR as a narrative medium.
