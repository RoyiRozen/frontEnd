The Both of Us VR Film

2020
VR Creative Video

Project Overview

The Both of Us is a 10-minute science fiction drama in Virtual Reality, created as my Digital Media MFA Graduate Final Project. Written and directed as a narrative VR experience, the film explores human connection within a technological dystopia through an immersive medium that challenges viewers to question their relationship with technology.

"It's a bit difficult from the shock and the intensity of the creation. I can feel it all over my body—the emotions of the actress, the feelings you wanted to convey. It's amazing to think you've chosen to use technology to criticize the same technology you are using—a choice of a genius."

Abstract

In a dystopian, dark and futuristic world, most of humanity is addicted to the ultimate escapist experience - fantastic virtual reality glasses. It is the flagship product of the huge corporation, TCR, which controls the entire world. Now, humanity's only hope is two friends, Omar and Mark, the last remnants of the underground resistance to the corporation, who reunite to eliminate TCR once and for all. Yet is this world even worth fighting for?

Cinematic VR Production

Creating a narrative VR film required a blend of traditional filmmaking techniques and cutting-edge virtual reality technology. We captured the movie scenes using photogrammetry from multiple sources, including laser scanners, drones, and handheld cameras, creating highly detailed 3D environments that viewers could explore in VR.

The production blended traditional filmmaking with cutting-edge virtual technologies. We used Unity for VR development, implementing custom shaders and lighting to create the dystopian atmosphere. The volumetric capture of actors allowed viewers to experience performances from multiple angles within the 3D space, creating a unique form of cinematic storytelling that leverages the immersive qualities of VR.

Creative Approach

The film uses the medium of VR to critique the very technology it employs, creating a meta-narrative that engages viewers in a dialogue about our relationship with technology. By immersing the audience in a dystopian world dominated by virtual reality, we invite them to experience the seductive power of technology while simultaneously reflecting on its potential dangers.

The narrative structure takes advantage of VR's unique capabilities, allowing viewers to experience the story from multiple perspectives and make their own connections between scenes and characters. This approach challenges traditional linear storytelling and creates a more personalized experience for each viewer.

Impact & Recognition

The Both of Us has been recognized for its innovative approach to VR storytelling and its thoughtful exploration of human connection in a technologically mediated world. The project has been screened at several VR film festivals and has contributed to ongoing conversations about the potential of VR as a narrative medium.

As my MFA graduate final project, it represents a culmination of my research into immersive storytelling and demonstrates my approach to using technology to create meaningful emotional experiences that prompt reflection on our society's relationship with technology.
