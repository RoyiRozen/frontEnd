Project Brief

The Caeserstone AR project transforms how customers visualize surface materials. This augmented reality app allows users to instantly see how different Caeserstone quartz surfaces would look in their actual spaces (countertops, backsplashes), eliminating guesswork. It features a digital catalog displayed with photorealistic accuracy. My role involved [User to specify role, e.g., lead AR development, UI/UX design].

Key Features & Technology

The app featured advanced surface detection (ARKit/ARCore), PBR materials for realism, custom shaders, and real-time lighting adaptation. Key functionalities included intelligent plane detection, surface calibration, measurement tools, and integration with Caeserstone's retail and sample ordering systems. The tech stack was centered around Unity and AR Foundation for cross-platform deployment.

Project Impact & Outcomes

The Caeserstone AR app significantly boosted customer confidence (43% increase) and sales conversion (28% increase), while reducing material sample requests. It gained over 250,000 active users in its first year and was adopted by 70% of the distribution network as a sales tool, becoming key to Caeserstone's digital strategy and winning industry awards.
