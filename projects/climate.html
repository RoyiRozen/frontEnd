Project Overview: Climate Change Here & Now

ClimateXR is a research project from Tel Aviv University's Immersive Media and Cognition group at the Sagol Brain Institute. We studied how immersive Virtual Reality (VR) affects psychological distance towards climate change and the willingness to act.

The Challenge: Psychological Distance

Israel is a climate change "Hotspot," facing hotter heat waves and coastal flooding. While solutions exist, psychological barriers like "Psychological Distance" hinder action. Events perceived as distant (temporally, geographically, or socially) receive less urgency.

Technical Implementation

ClimateXR used advanced tech for immersive simulations, built in Unity to simulate climate effects in Israel. Participants used VR headsets with neuro-feedback sensors to measure psychological and physiological responses, analyzing how immersion influenced their perception of climate change.

Research Methodology & Findings

Our mixed-methods approach combined qualitative and quantitative data. VR simulations effectively reduced psychological distance: participants felt climate change was closer and more urgent. The study highlighted VR's potential to foster pro-environmental attitudes and behaviors.

Key findings indicate that embodied VR experiences can make abstract threats like climate change feel more tangible and personally relevant. This underscores VR's power as a tool for science communication and advocacy.
