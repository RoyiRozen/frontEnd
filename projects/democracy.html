Democracy AR

AR 3D Dev Spatial Design Leadership Creative Hackathon

Democracy AR is a hackathon-winning augmented reality application that transforms street signs into powerful protest messages during Israel's 2023 judicial reform demonstrations. Created in 48 hours, this viral AR experience allowed protesters to visualize alternative realities and amplify their message through shareable digital activism.

AR Activism Innovation

Traditional protest methods often struggle to communicate complex constitutional issues to broader audiences. Democracy AR addressed this by creating an intuitive AR experience that replaced street signs with protest messages, making abstract concerns about judicial independence tangible and shareable. Users simply pointed their phones at street signs to see alternative messages like "Democracy Under Attack" or "Protect Our Courts," creating powerful visual metaphors that resonated across social media platforms and news coverage.

Viral Impact

The application achieved remarkable reach within its first week, attracting over 50,000 users and generating 20,000+ social media shares that amplified the protest message far beyond physical demonstrations. The AR experience provided compelling visual content that news outlets used to illustrate the protests, while the shareable nature of AR screenshots created organic viral spread across platforms. Most importantly, the app directly contributed to protest mobilization, with over 15,000 users registering for demonstrations through the integrated call-to-action features.

Technical Achievement

Built in just 48 hours using WebXR and Three.js for maximum accessibility, Democracy AR required no app downloads and worked directly in mobile browsers. The team developed custom computer vision algorithms for reliable street sign detection under varying lighting conditions, while ensuring the lightweight AR experience performed smoothly on older devices. The rapid development cycle and cross-platform compatibility were crucial to the app's viral success, allowing immediate adoption during the height of the protest movement without technical barriers.
