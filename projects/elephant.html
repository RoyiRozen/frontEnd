The Big Elephant Migration AR Art Exhibition

2022
AR Conservation Public Art

Project Overview

The Big Elephant Migration is an augmented reality art exhibition that brings the wonder and plight of African elephants to urban environments around the world. Using AR technology, this installation creates a virtual migration of elephants through city streets and public spaces, allowing viewers to encounter these magnificent creatures in unexpected contexts while raising awareness about elephant conservation.

By bringing elephants into urban spaces where they don't belong, we create a powerful metaphor for displacement and habitat loss. The stark juxtaposition of these majestic wild animals against concrete and glass provokes questions about our relationship with the natural world.

Concept and Vision

The exhibition emerged from two parallel concerns: the ongoing decline of elephant populations across Africa and the growing disconnection between urban populations and wildlife. By using augmented reality to create a virtual migration through urban environments, the project aims to:

  • Create emotional connections between city dwellers and elephants
  • Educate the public about elephant migration patterns and the threats they face
  • Demonstrate how technology can be used for conservation awareness
  • Challenge the separation between "human spaces" and "animal spaces"

Rather than presenting elephants as static displays in a traditional gallery, the AR format allows viewers to experience the dynamics of elephant family groups, their social behaviors, and their movement through space. The virtual elephants respond to their environment - navigating around real obstacles, interacting with each other, and even acknowledging the human viewers.

Educational Component

At its core, this project is as much about education as it is about art. The exhibition incorporates several key facts about elephant conservation:

50%
Decline in elephant populations across Africa over the past 70 years
16,000
Kilometers traveled annually by some elephant herds during migration
70
Years that elephants can live in the wild, developing complex social structures

The app that accompanies the exhibition provides additional information about elephant conservation, explaining the impact of habitat loss, poaching, and human-elephant conflict. It also highlights conservation success stories and directs viewers to reputable organizations working on elephant protection.

Technical Implementation

Creating a convincing augmented reality experience of elephants in urban spaces required sophisticated technical solutions:

ARKit & ARCore
3D Modeling
Spatial Mapping
Animation

The project was built in Unity using AR Foundation to ensure cross-platform compatibility between iOS and Android devices. We conducted extensive research on elephant movement and behavior, working with wildlife experts to create realistic animations and behaviors. Each elephant in the herd has unique characteristics and behaviors, powered by a custom AI system that directs their interactions with the environment and each other.

One of the biggest technical challenges was developing accurate occlusion - ensuring that the virtual elephants correctly pass behind real-world objects like trees or buildings. We implemented depth-sensing algorithms that allow the app to construct a 3D understanding of the physical space and properly layer the digital elephants within it.

The AR Experience

Visitors experience the exhibition by downloading a dedicated mobile application and following a predetermined route through public spaces. As they walk, they encounter different elephant scenarios:

Family Groups
Matriarch-led family units with calves protected by the group, demonstrating elephant social structure and care behaviors
Water Holes
Elephants gathering at virtual water sources (often placed in fountains or reflecting pools), showing their dependency on these critical resources
Migration Corridors
Long lines of elephants moving through urban streets, illustrating migration patterns and the challenges of fragmented habitats
Feeding Grounds
Elephants interacting with trees and vegetation in urban parks, demonstrating their role as ecosystem engineers

The AR experience is designed to be both educational and emotional. When visitors approach the virtual elephants, the animals acknowledge their presence - sometimes with curiosity, sometimes with wariness. This interactive element helps create a sense of connection between the viewer and the digital wildlife.

Exhibition Design

While the primary exhibition exists in augmented reality, the physical components of the installation are equally important:

  • Way-finding System: Physical markers throughout the exhibition route help guide visitors and provide clear locations for AR triggers
  • Information Panels: Traditional information displays that provide context and encourage app downloads
  • Sound Design: Location-specific audio played through visitors' devices enhances the immersive experience
  • Community Engagement: Interactive elements that allow visitors to "leave their mark" by contributing to a collective digital artwork

The exhibition is designed to be adaptable to different urban environments. It has been installed in public parks, along commercial streets, and in urban squares, with each location offering a unique context for the elephant migration narrative.

Impact and Future Development

Since its initial launch, The Big Elephant Migration has been exhibited in several cities worldwide, reaching thousands of visitors. Feedback has indicated that the exhibition creates meaningful emotional responses and increased interest in elephant conservation among participants.

The project continues to evolve with planned expansions including:

  • Additional species of endangered wildlife to highlight broader conservation issues
  • Collaborative features allowing multiple visitors to share the same AR experience simultaneously
  • Integration with conservation donation platforms to convert engagement into direct action
  • Educational curriculum development for school groups visiting the exhibition

The Big Elephant Migration demonstrates how augmented reality can bridge the gap between art, education, and conservation, creating immersive experiences that inspire action and awareness.
