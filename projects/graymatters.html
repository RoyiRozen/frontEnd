Project Overview

Gray Matters Health is a pioneering healthcare technology company focused on developing innovative treatments for mental health conditions. I collaborated with their team to design and implement the visual experience for "Reward Loop," a revolutionary treatment for PTSD that utilizes neurofeedback from a brain-computer interface.

The Challenge

PTSD affects millions worldwide, with traditional treatments often showing limited effectiveness. GrayMatters Health developed a groundbreaking approach using neurofeedback to help patients gain voluntary control over emotion regulation areas of their brain—regions typically dysregulated in PTSD.

The challenge was to create a visual experience that would:

  • Translate complex neurofeedback signals into intuitive visual feedback
  • Engage patients in a way that maintained focus throughout treatment sessions
  • Provide a sense of achievement and progress to reinforce positive outcomes
  • Create a calming, non-triggering environment for PTSD patients

My Role

As the Experience Designer for this project, I was responsible for creating the visual feedback interface that patients would interact with during their neurofeedback sessions. My work included:

  • Conceptualizing the visual representation of neural activity
  • Designing responsive visual elements that react in real-time to brain signals
  • Implementing the visual design in Unity using VFX Graph for dynamic particle effects
  • Creating a progression system that showed improvement over multiple sessions
  • Collaborating with neuroscientists to ensure the visual feedback aligned with the therapeutic goals

The Reward Loop

How It Works

The Reward Loop is a self-reinforcing cycle that helps patients gain control over their brain activity related to emotion regulation. The process creates a positive feedback loop that strengthens neural pathways through visual reinforcement.

Technical Implementation

The visual experience was built using several advanced technologies to create a responsive and engaging interface:

Unity
VFX Graph

BCI Integration

I utilized Unity's VFX Graph to create dynamic particle systems that responded directly to neurofeedback data. This allowed for smooth, real-time visual feedback that could adapt to subtle changes in brain activity. The implementation included custom shaders for visual effects and C# scripts to handle the integration with GrayMatters' proprietary brain-computer interface.

Design Process

The design process involved close collaboration with neuroscientists, clinicians, and patients to create a visual experience that was both scientifically valid and therapeutically effective:

  1. Research: Studied the neuroscience of PTSD and how visual feedback could influence brain activity
  2. Conceptualization: Developed multiple visual concepts based on research findings
  3. Prototyping: Created interactive prototypes to test different visual approaches
  4. Testing: Conducted user testing with clinicians and volunteer participants
  5. Refinement: Iterated on the design based on feedback and performance metrics
  6. Implementation: Built the final solution in Unity with VFX Graph
  7. Clinical Integration: Supported the integration into the clinical workflow

Outcomes & Impact

The Reward Loop visual experience has become an integral part of GrayMatters Health's PTSD treatment protocol. Early clinical results have shown promising outcomes, with patients reporting improved engagement with the therapy and clinicians noting enhanced ability to monitor and guide patients through the treatment process.

The project demonstrates the potential of combining neuroscience, technology, and thoughtful design to create new approaches to mental health treatment. By translating complex neurological processes into intuitive visual feedback, we've helped make an innovative therapy more accessible and effective for patients suffering from PTSD.
