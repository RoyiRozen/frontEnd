Project Overview

MemoriAR is a response to the Hamas attack on Israel on October 7th, 2023. In the face of tragedy, we harnessed our technical expertise to create something meaningful—a memorial platform that enables family members to cherish and preserve the memories of their loved ones through augmented reality.

A New Way to Keep Memories Alive: MemoriAR represents a profound reimagining of how we preserve and honor memories, allowing families to create persistent, location-based augmented reality memorials that can be visited and experienced by anyone with a mobile device.

Technical Implementation

The MemoriAR platform was built using Unity as our primary development environment. To create compelling and respectful memorial experiences, we combined several technologies:

Unity
Blender
ARCore
Geospatial API

We used Blender for creating 3D assets and animations that represented memories in a tasteful and meaningful way. These assets were then integrated into Unity to create the augmented reality experience. The Google Geospatial API enabled us to accurately place memorials in specific locations, allowing visitors to experience them exactly where they were intended to be.

This proof of concept demonstrates how AR can be used not just for entertainment or commercial purposes, but as a powerful medium for remembrance and healing.

Features

  • Location-Based Memorials: Place digital memorials at meaningful physical locations
  • Memory Artifacts: Upload photos, videos, and 3D objects that represent the person's life
  • Personal Stories: Record and share stories and memories that visitors can experience
  • Collective Remembrance: Allow visitors to leave messages or tokens of remembrance
  • Persistent Experience: Memorials remain accessible indefinitely, creating a lasting tribute

Impact & Recognition

MemoriAR was presented at the Google Geospatial Creator Hackathon, where it was recognized for its innovative approach to memory preservation and its sensitive response to a real-world tragedy. The project received significant interest from community organizations and memorial foundations.

While initially conceived as a response to the October 7th attacks, the platform has the potential to serve as a universal tool for remembrance, applicable to various contexts where preserving personal and collective memory is important.

Future Development

Moving forward, we are exploring partnerships with memorial institutions and community organizations to expand the reach and impact of MemoriAR. Key areas of development include:

  • Enhanced content creation tools for families with limited technical expertise
  • Integration with historical archives and databases
  • Multilingual support to serve diverse communities
  • Accessibility features to ensure everyone can experience the memorials
